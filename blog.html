How AI is Reshaping the Future of Online Education

Personalised learning paths, instant feedback, AI tutors — the classroom of 2030 is being built today. Here's what you need to know.

The Science of Retaining What You Learn

Why do we forget 70% of what we read within 24 hours — and what actually works to fix it?

👤 Sara Pillai📅 Mar 2025☕ 5 min read

The Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve shows that without reinforcement, we lose most new information within a day. But the research also shows exactly how to fight back.

Spaced repetition is the most evidence-backed method: revisit material at increasing intervals (day 1, day 3, day 7, day 21). Apps like Anki make this automatic.

Active recall — testing yourself rather than re-reading — is twice as effective as passive review. Close the textbook and try to write down everything you know.

Teach it: explaining a concept to someone else (or even to a rubber duck) forces you to fill in gaps in your understanding.

How to Stay Consistent When Learning Online

The dropout rate for online courses is 90%. Here's how to be in the 10% that finishes.

👤 James Teo📅 Feb 2025☕ 3 min read

The biggest mistake online learners make is relying on motivation. Motivation is a feeling — it comes and goes. What actually works is systems.

Set a non-negotiable time block: 30 minutes every morning before breakfast. Attach learning to an existing habit (habit stacking). Track your streak visually — a simple calendar with X marks is surprisingly powerful.

Find accountability: a study partner, a Discord community, or even publicly committing to your goal on LinkedIn. And remember: done is better than perfect. A 20-minute session beats a skipped 2-hour session every time.
